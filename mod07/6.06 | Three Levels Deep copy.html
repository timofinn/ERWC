6.06 | Three Levels Deep

✦ Skill: Building Comfort · Working a Progression · Earning Depth

Good interviewers don't improvise randomly — they work a progression. Surface first, then detail, then depth. You don't skip levels. Level 3 only works if Levels 1 and 2 did their job first.

The Structure

Three rounds, each going one level deeper. You switch roles after every round — both people practice asking and answering at each level.

The One Rule

Your next question must connect to what your partner just said. No pivoting. No topic-jumping. No going back to your list. Stay in the conversation.

The Reference

Your 6.06 Reference has a full question bank at each level. Use it if you get stuck — but try without it first. The goal is to internalize the arc, not read from a list.

The progression

Level 1

Surface

Safe, open-ended openers. Low stakes. You're setting a tone: I'm interested. This is comfortable. We can talk.

No pressure. No depth yet. Just get it moving.

Level 2

Detail

Rapport is established. Now gather specifics — concrete moments, feelings, decisions. Still focused on what happened, not what it meant.

Who was there? What happened first? Walk me through it.

Level 3

Depth

Reserved for when your subject has shown they're ready. Values, regret, belief, identity. You earn your way here — you can't parachute in.

What did it mean? What did it change?

The three rounds

Round 1 — Level 1: Surface

3 min each

Speaker

Pick any topic — something low-stakes. Talk freely. Give your partner something to work with. Keep going even if it feels like small talk.

Questioner

Level 1 only — open, safe, low-stakes. Your job right now is to set a tone, not to go deep. Let the conversation move naturally. No judgment. No big questions. Just: I'm interested. Tell me more.

📝 Track it: What Level 1 question worked best? Why do you think it opened things up?

🔁 Switch roles. Repeat Round 1 before moving on.

Round 2 — Level 2: Detail

4 min each

Speaker

New topic, or pick up where Round 1 left off. Stay in it — keep talking even when the questions push you somewhere unexpected. That unexpected place is usually the interesting one.

Questioner

Move to Level 2. You've established enough comfort — now get the concrete picture. Ask for specifics: Who was there? What happened first? Walk me through it. What was that like day to day? Still focused on what happened, not what it meant. No judgment.

📝 Track it: Which question got you the most specific answer? Write it here.

🔁 Switch roles. Repeat Round 2 before moving on.

Round 3 — Level 3: Depth

5 min each

Speaker

Same topic from Round 2 if possible. Your partner is going deeper now — stay with it. This is where the real conversation happens. If you feel yourself pulling back, notice that and keep going anyway.

Questioner

You've earned this. Now ask what it meant, not just what happened. Try: "Looking back, was that the right call — or is it more complicated than that?" / "What did that period teach you about what you actually value?" / "What do you wish you'd known going into it?" If they pause — wait. The real answer usually comes after the first one.

📝 Track it: Write the Level 3 question you asked. What did it open up — or why didn't it land?

🔁 Switch roles. Repeat Round 3.

Going deeper

What to do when you get a flat answer +

A short or closed response doesn't mean your subject is done — it usually means you moved too fast. Don't skip ahead to Level 3 when the conversation stalls. Slow down and go wider at the level you're at. Ask something adjacent: a different moment, a different person who was involved, a different angle on the same story. Get more surface before you try to go deep. Depth that hasn't been earned just feels like an interrogation.

Why silence is still your best tool +

Level 3 questions land differently than Level 1 and 2 — they ask people to say something true about themselves, which takes a second. If your subject pauses after a depth question, that pause is a good sign. Don't fill it. Don't reword the question. Don't offer them an out. Sit in it. The answer that comes after the pause is almost always better than the one that would've come without it.

How this connects to 6.08 +

In your final interview your subject won't announce when they're ready for a deeper question. You'll have to read it — the moment the conversation opens up enough that a Level 3 question won't feel like a left turn. Today you practice that arc in a controlled setting so the instinct becomes automatic. By 6.08, you shouldn't be thinking about which level you're at. You should just be listening.
