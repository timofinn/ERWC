This week you're watching five graduation speeches — all written and delivered by people your age. Your job is to watch them like a writer: figure out what they're doing, decide if it works, and figure out what you might steal. The handout packet has all the questions. This page is your map.

The Four Lenses

Every speech this week fits at least one of these formats. Knowing the format before you watch is what separates a writer watching from an audience member just sitting there.

7.02

Humor → Sincere

Builds rapport through jokes, then pivots to something real. The humor has to earn the sincerity — if it doesn't, the pivot just feels random.

7.03

Nostalgia → Motivational

Walks the audience through shared memory, then turns it into a call to move forward. The past has to feel like setup, not just warmup.

7.04

Identity-Centered

Personal difference becomes universal insight. The "I" has to do broad work — or it's just a speech about yourself.

7.05

Risqué → Critical

Uses the platform to name something uncomfortable. The speaker is taking a real risk in front of a real audience — and paying a real price for it.
