7.14  |  Graduation Speech

Say It Out Loud

The speech lives on a stage, not a screen. Today you get off the page — read it aloud, hear what works, get feedback, and record.

Today's Plan

Three Rounds

Fifty-five minutes. Here's the shape:

Round 1 — Solo Read-Aloud
0–15 min

Read your draft out loud to yourself. Not in your head — actually out loud. Time it.

  • Mark any line that feels awkward to say. Put a star in the margin.
  • Notice where you speed up. That's usually a sign you don't trust the line.
  • Note the total time. Under 4 minutes? Add a beat. Over 6? Find 30 seconds to cut.
Target: 4–6 minutes at your actual speaking pace. If you can't hit that today, that's what revision is for.
Round 2 — Partner Listen
15–40 min

Find a partner. Read your speech to them — not at them. Put the paper down as much as you can. Your partner's job is to listen, not follow along.

After each reading, use the feedback table below. Switch roles. Be specific.

Round 3 — Final Pass + Record
40–55 min

Take five minutes to fix anything that came up. Then record yourself reading the full speech. One take is fine. Two if you need it.

  • Use your phone, a Chromebook camera, or whatever's available.
  • Stand up if you can. It changes the delivery.
  • Don't start over because you stumbled. Real speeches have stumbles. Keep going.
Partner Feedback

Listen. Then Tell the Truth.

Good feedback is specific. "It was good" tells them nothing. "The part about the hallway made me remember something" — that's something they can use.

What to notice What to say to your partner
The hook Tell them what image or idea stuck with you in the first 30 seconds. Did it make you want to keep listening?
The moment Name the specific scene or detail you remember most. If you can't name one, the story might not be landing yet.
The turn Point to the line where it felt like it stopped being just their story. Did that shift happen?
The close What's the last thing you actually heard? Did it feel like it ended, or just stopped?
Delivery One moment they made eye contact or slowed down and it worked. One moment you lost them to the paper.
Recording Checklist

Before You Hit Record

You don't need a studio. You need a quiet-ish spot and a way to see yourself or hear yourself back.

  • Find a spot with decent sound — not right next to an open window or a loud HVAC.
  • Stand up, or sit up straight. Slouching changes your voice more than you think.
  • Have your Google Doc open but try not to read every word. You can glance — don't stare.
  • Record at least the full speech, start to finish, without stopping.
  • Watch it back or listen once. You don't have to be happy with it. You just have to submit it.
