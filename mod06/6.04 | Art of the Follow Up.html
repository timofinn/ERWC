What you're doing today

The Setup

You and a partner take turns sharing. The listener's only job is to respond with a question that grows directly from what was just said — no topic jumps, nothing pre-planned.

The Rule

If your question could have been asked before your partner opened their mouth, it doesn't count. It has to come from something they actually said.

Why It Matters

Most people ask the question they planned while the other person was still talking. A follow-up question proves you were tracking — not just waiting.

Three rounds

Round

What you do

The constraint

Round 1

Paraphrase only. Reflect back what your partner said in your own words before asking anything.

No questions yet. Prove you heard it first.

Round 2

Ask one follow-up question. It has to come from something specific your partner said — not a topic you wanted to explore.

One question only. Make it count.

Round 3

Full sequence: paraphrase first, then one follow-up question, then let silence land before moving on.

Don't fill the silence. Let it work.

Going deeper

What a real follow-up sounds like +

Your partner says: "I almost quit that job three times but something kept pulling me back."

Weak follow-up: "What kind of work were you doing?" — this ignores everything they just said.

Strong follow-up: "What was the thing that kept pulling you back?" — stays inside the moment they opened.

The strong version only works if you were actually listening. That's the whole point.

Why silence is part of the follow-up +

After a good follow-up question, the instinct is to keep talking — add a thought, soften it, explain yourself. Don't. The question landed. Let it sit. Most people will fill the silence with something more honest than what they would have said if you'd kept moving. Silence is not dead air — it's the follow-up doing its job.

How this connects to 6.08 +

In your final interview, you'll have a question progression prepared. But the best moments in any interview come from abandoning it — from hearing something your subject said and following it somewhere you didn't plan to go. That only happens if you've built the habit of listening for what's actually being said. That habit starts here.
