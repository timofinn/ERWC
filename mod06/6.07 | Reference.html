6.07 | Example Questions by Theme

✦ Reference Only — Use as a spark, not a script

These questions are drawn from real conversations. They're organized by theme and sorted by level — but they're not a script. Adapt them to fit your specific person, your topic, and your own voice. If a question sounds like a form, rewrite it.

Connect to your guiding question

Every question you borrow from here should earn its place by connecting back to what you're actually trying to understand about this specific person.

Reference details specific to this person

The best questions aren't generic. Replace the brackets with real specifics — their job, their community, their story — before you ask anything.

Use photos

Before your interview, ask if they have any photos from the period you'll discuss. Have them walk you through the contents and the stories behind them. Photos unlock memory in a way questions alone can't.

L1 Surface L2 Detail L3 Depth — most themes move through all three. Start at L1.

Before your interview

Get permission to record.

Ask beforehand. Record audio on your phone. This is your primary source for transcription — don't rely on memory alone.

Ask about photos.

Before you arrive, ask if they have photos from the period you'll discuss. Have them walk you through what they see.

Know your questions cold.

So you can ignore them when the conversation needs it. The best moments come from following what they actually say.
