Interview Questioning Framework

Level 1
Reporting
(The "What")
Questions that ask for factual snapshots, biographical data, or specific sensory memories. The respondent reports — they don't have to interpret or evaluate.
"Where were you when you heard?" or "How did you feel in that moment?"
Level 2
Analyzing
(The "How/Why")
Questions that ask the respondent to connect dots, compare events, or explain the mechanics of an emotion — while still inside the story's timeline.
"How did those feelings change over the next week?" or "Why did you choose that treatment over the other?"
Level 3
Meaning-Making
(The "So What")
Questions that require a "temporal shift" — asking the respondent to step outside the story and look back from the present to evaluate their identity, wisdom, or what the experience made true.
"Looking back, how did that reshape who you are?" or "What does being 'brave' mean to you now?"
