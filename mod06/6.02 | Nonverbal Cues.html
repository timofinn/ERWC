Unit 6 | Narrative Medicine

6.02  ·  Nonverbal Cues

Read the Room

Your body is always talking. Today you find out what it's been saying — and what it does to the person across from you.

What you're doing today

The Setup

One person tells a story. The other performs nonverbal cues from the reference sheet — one at a time, on a signal. The speaker keeps talking through all of it.

Your Job

Notice what shifts when the cue changes. As the speaker — what did you feel like doing? As the listener — what were you communicating without knowing it?

Why It Matters

You can close someone down before you ask a single question. You can also open them up. Knowing the difference — and being able to control it — is what this lesson is about.

The two roles

Role

What you do

What you're tracking

Speaker

Tell a real story — something that happened, something you remember. Keep talking through each cue, even when it gets uncomfortable.

What you feel like doing when each cue hits. Do you want to stop? Speed up? Look away?

Listener

Move through cues from the reference sheet one at a time, on the teacher's signal. Stay silent — no words, just body.

Which cues are hardest to hold. Which ones you default to without thinking.

Switch roles after three rounds. Everyone gets both sides of the table.

Going deeper

The cues you'll work through +

A sample from the reference sheet — you'll get the full list in class:

  • Full eye contact, open posture, slight lean forward
  • Eyes drifting, body turned slightly away
  • Arms crossed, face neutral
  • Nodding slowly, mirroring the speaker's energy
  • Looking at hands while the speaker talks

Each one produces a different experience for the speaker. That's the whole point.

What good listening looks like in a body +

There's no single right posture — but there's a recognizable cluster. Good listeners tend to:

  • Stay still instead of fidgeting
  • Match energy without mirroring artificially
  • Keep their face responsive without performing surprise
  • Let silence land instead of rushing to fill it

Most of this is unconscious. Today you're making it conscious — so you can actually control it.

How this connects to 6.08 +

A subject who feels genuinely listened to talks more, goes deeper, and trusts you with the real material. In 6.08, you don't get to control what your subject does — but you get to control what you do. Your body is either helping the conversation happen or getting in the way. This lesson is where you figure out which.
